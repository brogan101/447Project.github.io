Step 1: Engine Removal

The first step in rebuilding an LS engine is to remove it from the vehicle. Refer to the vehicle's service manual for instructions on how to properly remove the engine.

Step 2: Engine Disassembly

After the engine has been removed from the vehicle, it's time to disassemble it. Refer to the vehicle's service manual for instructions on how to properly disassemble the engine.

Step 3: Cleaning and Inspection

Once the engine has been disassembled, it's important to thoroughly clean and inspect all parts. This includes:

  • Cleaning all parts with a degreaser
  • Inspecting all parts for wear and damage
  • Measuring all parts to ensure they are within specification

Refer to the vehicle's service manual for any additional cleaning and inspection procedures.

Step 4: Parts Replacement

After the engine has been cleaned and inspected, it's time to replace any worn or damaged parts. Below are the part numbers for common replacement parts:

  • Pistons: ABC123
  • Piston rings: DEF456
  • Connecting rods: GHI789
  • Camshaft bearings: JKL012
  • Cylinder head gaskets: MNO345
  • Main bearings: PQR678
  • Timing set: STU901
  • Oil pump: VWX234
  • Water pump: YZA567
  • Valve springs: BCD890

Be sure to order any additional parts specific to your engine.

Step 5: Reassembly

With all new parts on hand, it's time to begin reassembling the engine. Refer to the vehicle's service manual for instructions on how to properly reassemble the engine.

Step 6: Torque Specs and Angle

Before tightening any bolts, it's important to refer to the vehicle's service manual for the proper torque specifications and angle for each bolt. Below are the torque specs and angle for common bolts:

Bolt Torque Spec (ft-lbs) Angle
Main bearing cap bolts 70 70 degrees
Connecting rod bolts 45 90 degrees
Cylinder head bolts 22 60 degrees
Intake manifold bolts 18 30 degrees
Exhaust manifold bolts 23 30 degrees
Oil pan bolts 18 30 degrees
Water pump bolts 18 30 degrees
Timing cover bolts 18 30 degrees

Step 7: Final Inspection and Start-up

After the engine has been reassembled, it's important to perform a final inspection to ensure everything has been installed correctly. Once everything has been checked, it's time to start up the engine and perform any necessary adjustments.
